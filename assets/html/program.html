The SBA offers a variety of loans.

There are many types of loans that you can receive from or in conjunction with the SBA. Below is a list of the most common.

Disaster Loans

These loans are available for businesses of all sizes located in declared disaster areas, including private and nonprofit organizations, homeowners, and renters affected by declared disasters, including civil unrest and natural disasters such as hurricanes, flooding, and wildfires. They can be used for losses not covered by insurance or funding from the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA), and for business operating expenses that could have been met had the disaster not occurred.

Covid Relief Loans

At this time, we are no longer accepting applications for Covid Relief loans, including the Paycheck Protection Program, EIDL, Shuttered Venues Grant, or the Restaurant Revitalization Fund.

Microloans

The microloan program provides loans up to $50,000 to help small businesses and certain not-for-profit childcare centers start up and expand. The average microloan is about $13,000. Microloans can be used for a variety of purposes that help small businesses expand. Use them when you need less than $50,000 to rebuild, re-open, repair, enhance, or improve your small business.

7(a) Loans

The 7(a) Loan Program, SBA’s most common loan program, includes financial help for small businesses with special requirements. This is the best option when real estate is part of a business purchase, but it can also be used for Short- and long-term working capital; Refinance current business debt; and/or Purchase furniture, fixtures, and supplies. The maximum loan amount for a 7(a) loan is $5 million.  

504 Loans

The CDC/504 Loan Program provides long-term, fixed rate financing of up to $5 million for major fixed assets that promote business growth and job creation. A 504 loan can be used for a range of assets, including the purchase or construction of existing buildings or land, new facilities, long-term machinery and equipment; OR the improvement or modernization of land, streets, utilities, parking lots and landscaping or existing facilities.
