You are eligible for this program if your business...

  • Is a small business as defined by the SBA industry guidelines
  • Is registered in the US as a sole proprietorship, LLC, Corporation, or S-Corp. Nonprofits and Trusts are not currently supported at this time.
  • You are looking for funding under $100,000.
  • You are looking for funding for: working capital, equipment, payroll, starting a business, orÂ disaster funds.
  • While having a bankruptcy or poor credit may limit what we can offer, they currently do not exclude you from this program!

You are ineligible for this program if your business...

  • Firms engaged in activities that are prohibited by federal law or applicable law in the jurisdiction where the business is located or conducted.

    Examples include but are not limited to:

    • The sale or distribution of illegal drugs (note this includes cannabis, as it is not legal at the federal level, even if it is for your state)
    • Prostitution, sex work, or other similar activities
    • Included in these activities are the production, servicing, or distribution of otherwise legal products that are to be used in connection with an illegal activity, such as selling drug paraphernalia or operating a motel that permits illegal prostitution
    • Theft, fraud, and financial crimes
    • Among others. Please talk to a lawyer and read our terms and conditions for more information.
    • Note: If the business owner has been arrested, they are not necessarily barred from getting a loan; it may, however, take longer to confirm your loan. Those on parole are not currently eligible.
  • Businesses in the following industries are currently not eligible:
    • Corporate-owned franchises
    • Pay day loan stores
    • Pawn shops
    • Astrology, palm reading
    • Adult bookstores, strip clubs
    • Track waging facilities
